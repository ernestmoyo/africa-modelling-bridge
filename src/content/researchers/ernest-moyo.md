Ernest Moyo is the founder of the Africa Modelling Bridge and the modeller who drives its
narrative. He sits at the intersection of **mathematical modelling, public-health epidemiology and
actuarial science** — a combination built to answer the question every health system now faces:
*with less money, where does it go, and how do we make the case for more?*

### A decade inside the ministry rooms

For over ten years Ernest has worked with governments, multilateral partners and research
institutions across **Southern and East Africa**, supporting Ministries of Health on surveillance,
analytics, and the interpretation of modelling outputs into decisions.

- **Regional Epidemiologist, Clinton Health Access Initiative (CHAI), Southern Africa.** Led
  retrospective analyses and transmission-driver studies for malaria in Angola, Namibia and
  Zimbabwe; supported **risk mapping and stratification as the interface with national malaria
  programmes (NMCPs) in Zimbabwe, Angola, South Africa and Mozambique, in partnership with the
  Malaria Atlas Project (MAP) and Swiss TPH**; designed and piloted Focus Investigations in Namibia
  for high-risk case response; led intervention evaluations across CHAI-supported countries.
- **Malaria Elimination Programme Officer, CHAI Zimbabwe.** Built elimination surveillance systems
  and led the first DHIS2 tracker rollout for case and focus investigations, providing technical
  oversight for the Ministry of Health from provincial to ward level.
- **Senior Data Scientist, PAMCA**, and **Head of Research, Monitoring & Evaluation** at Delta
  Philanthropies / Higherlife Foundation — M&E systems across Zimbabwe, Lesotho, Kenya and Burundi.

### Sharpening the craft

Ernest is now completing a **PhD in Applied Mathematics & Computational Science** at the Nelson
Mandela African Institution of Science and Technology (NM-AIST), embedded in the **Vector Atlas**
consortium. His doctoral work is **EpiWave** — a Bayesian geostatistical framework for
high-resolution malaria risk mapping that integrates vector distribution, insecticide resistance and
surveillance into pixel-level, decision-ready maps for national programmes. He has also taken up
regional modelling training and capacity-building, including in Kenya, and supported countries such
as Namibia in strengthening their risk-stratification work.

Trained in **actuarial science** (BSc), with an **MSc in Biostatistics & Epidemiology**, he brings a
rare second lens — *health financing and sustainability* — to the modelling: forecasting funding
gaps, assessing fiscal space, and designing the investment cases that turn a model into money.

> The Bridge exists because Ernest has lived the gap it closes — the distance between a model in a
> university and a decision in a ministry. He is building the movement to shorten it.
